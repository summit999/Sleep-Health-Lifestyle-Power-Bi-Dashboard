## üìÇ Dataset

---

## üìä Dashboard pages

1. **Executive summary (Page 1)**
   - **KPIs:** Average sleep duration, stress level, daily steps, physical activity, and sleep quality.
   - **Disorder overview:** Pie chart showing None vs Insomnia vs Sleep Apnea.
   - **Key highlights:** Quick callouts of high‚Äërisk segments and notable trends for decision makers.

2. **Demographics & disorder (Page 2)**
   - **By age:** Line/area trends for sleep duration and quality across age groups.
   - **By gender:** Distribution of sleep disorders split by Male vs Female.
   - **By occupation:** Sleep quality and disorder prevalence across roles (Doctor, Nurse, Engineer, Teacher, etc.).

3. **Health analysis (Page 3)**
   - **BMI & disorders:** Counts of disorders by BMI category (Normal, Overweight, Obese, Underweight).
   - **Blood pressure & heart rate:** Summary visuals to contextualize cardiovascular metrics alongside sleep outcomes.
   - **Risk indicators:** Conditional formatting to flag combinations linked to apnea/insomnia.

4. **Lifestyle & activity (Page 4)**
   - **Stress vs sleep quality:** Scatter plot to show association and outliers.
   - **Physical activity vs sleep quality:** Line/curve indicating trend strength.
   - **Daily steps by occupation:** Treemap/bar to compare activity levels across professions.
   - **Sleep duration vs quality:** Relationship view to connect habits with outcomes.

---

## üîë Key Insights
- **Obesity and high stress** are strong predictors of sleep disorders.  
- **Doctors and Engineers** show healthier sleep patterns compared to Nurses and Sales Representatives.  
- **Sleep duration decreases with age**, while sleep disorders increase.  
- **Gender distribution** is fairly balanced, but lifestyle factors differ.
